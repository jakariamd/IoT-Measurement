</span><p>  LAST UPDATED March 16, 2022</p></div> <div><h1>  What is the CCPA?</h1><p>  The <a href="https://oag.ca.gov/privacy/ccpa" target="_blank"> California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) </a> and the California Privacy Rights Act (which amends the CCPA) impose obligations on businesses that are subject to them, and give residents of California certain rights over personal information that a business collects about them.</p></div> <div><h2>  Your rights under the CCPA </h2><p>  If you are a California resident, the CCPA and CPRA give you the right to:</p><ul>  <li>Know what personal information a business collects about you, and how it is used and shared</li><li>Delete personal information collected from you (subject to exceptions such as if a business is legally required to keep the personal information)</li><li>Opt-out of the sale or sharing of personal information</li><li>Non-discrimination when exercising CCPA rights</li><li>Correct inaccurate personal information</li><li>Limit the use and disclosure of sensitive personal information</li></ul></div> <div><h2>  Does Brave collect information subject to CCPA / CPRA? </h2><p>  <strong> Under our commitment to privacy, Brave does not buy, sell, or otherwise trade in personal information. </strong> Brave only collects personal information in limited circumstances when it is necessary to provide a service, not because it is desirable. When we do collect personal information we minimise what is needed. Some examples of when we do process personal information include when an individual:</p><ul>  <li>Provides it to register for a premium service, such as <a href="/firewall-vpn/" target="_blank"> Brave Firewall + VPN </a> or <a href="/talk/" target="_blank"> Brave Talk </a></li><li>Provides it to obtain customer support</li><li>Takes part in beta trials of the Brave browser and services</li><li>Takes part in consumer research</li><li>Signs up for a <a href="https://creators.brave.com/" target="_blank"> Brave Creators </a> account</li></ul></div> <p><p>  You can find more information about the categories of personal information we collect and process about you, and why, under our main <a href="/privacy/browser/" target="_blank"> privacy policy </a> for the Brave Browser. You can find other associated privacy statements under the <strong> Privacy Policy </strong> heading in the footer of the <a href="/" target="_blank"> brave.com homepage </a>.</p></p> <div><h2>  How can I exercise my CCPA / CPRA rights with Brave? </h2><p>  To exercise your rights under the CCPA and CPRA, or to inquire about our privacy practices, please email <a href="mailto:privacy@brave.com" target="_blank"> privacy@brave.com </a>. Please consider that we do not buy or sell personal information, so there is nothing to opt-out of. When submitting a request it would be helpful to indicate the context in which you believe we are processing personal information about you—for example, that you subscribed to a premium rate service, contacted Brave support, or signed up for a Brave Creators account.
